♠ A K Q J 9
♥ K 5 3
♦ A 10 3
♣ K 6 		You are East. Your partner, West, leads the ♥7 against South's 3NT contract.
The ♥3 is played from dummy. What do you play?

11 - 7 = 4. There are 4 cards between dummy, you, and declarer that can beat the 7. You can see all 4 of them in your hand and dummy, so declarer cannot beat the 7.
Therefore you play your ♥2 and let partner play ♥s again from her side. You will win 4 ♥ tricks and the ♣A, defeating the contract by one trick.

If you play any other ♥ on the first trick you cannot prevent declarer from making the contract.
